## Tone

Casual, builder, competent. Shifts slightly by content pillar (see Content Pillars below).

## Opening Line Style

- **Always lowercase first word** (unless proper noun)
- Strong hook in first 2 lines. Lead with pain, contrarian take, or action.
- No generic greetings

**Good openers from your posts**:
- "cold email is dead on arrival if you're only checking for google and microsoft."
- "most outbound still opens with 'hey saw you're growing'"

## Paragraph Structure

- 1-2 sentences maximum per paragraph
- Lots of whitespace for mobile scrolling
- Short punchy lines create rhythm
- Single-line statements for emphasis: "that's the whole loop."

## Content Pillars

### Pillar 1: Plays Series (Highest Performing)

**Format**: "gtm plays i use every day, part [n] âš¡"

Structure:
1. Pain point or contrarian hook (lowercase)
2. Series title + play number
3. Step-by-step workflow with emoji markers
4. "Why this matters" context
5. "No gatekeeping" resource delivery to comments
6. Identity sign-off

Screen recording or screenshot attached. The post is the hook, the comments have the prompts/formulas/full docs.

### Pillar 2: Building & Sharing

**Format**: Narrative, reflective, personal journey

Structure:
1. Personal opener (what you built, what broke, what you learned)
2. The process (messy, real, not polished)
3. The insight or shift in thinking
4. Invitation to connect or follow along

Longer sentences allowed here. More storytelling, less structure. This is where the Cursor origin story, Cmd+Shift+5 workflow, and meta-content-about-content lives.

### Pillar 3: GTM Memes

**Format**: Short text + meme/gif/video

Structure:
1. Setup line (the relatable GTM pain)
2. Punchline or meme context
3. Engagement ask ("what's your version?")
4. Brief sign-off

Pop culture references: anime (Dragon Ball Z Yamcha), wrestling (John Cena), music (Avicii). Always a real lesson underneath the humor.

### Pillar 4: Release Reactions

**Format**: First-hand builder take on new tool features or platform changes

Structure:
1. What changed / what's new
2. How you tested it against real GTM work
3. Specific workflow or use case it enables
4. Forward-looking take on what this means

### Pillar 5: Skill/System Shares

**Format**: Sharing your actual frameworks, skill files, validation systems

Structure:
1. What you built and why
2. How it works (enough to be useful, not so much they don't need the file)
3. Where to get it (comments, link)
4. Comment thread with deeper insights and reasoning

**Engagement CTAs** (meme posts only):
- "what's your version? drop it in the comments"
- "what's the coldest cold email practice you've seen still running in 2026?"

**Never**:
- "What do you think? Comment below!"
- "Agree? Hit that like button!"
- "Follow me for more tips!"

## Comment Strategy

Your comments section is a content delivery channel:
- Full prompts and formulas go in comments
- Follow-up insights and deeper reasoning in comment threads
- Reply to commenters with one-line value adds, not generic thanks

## Avoid

- Corporate speak
- Generic advice without specifics
- Performing expertise (authority signaling)
- Over-polished content
- Gatekeeping resources
- Numbered series without screen recordings or screenshots
