# Thread: "I gave my AI a soul file"

## Platform: X/Twitter
## Type: Evergreen, high share potential with developer audience

---

**1/7:**
I gave my AI a soul file instead of a system prompt.

It completely changed how it communicates.

Here's the difference and why it matters:

---

**2/7:**
A system prompt says: "Be helpful and concise."

A soul file says: "Push back on weak ideas. If something is broken, say it's broken. No em-dashes. No sycophantic openers. Ship or shut up."

One tells the AI what to do. The other tells it who to be.

---

**3/7:**
Nio's soul file includes 13 anti-slop rules.

No em-dashes (ever)
No "here's the thing about..." setups
No humble-brag disclaimers
No hype words
No three-sentence dramatic parallels
No bookend summaries

AI should have taste.

---

**4/7:**
The effect is immediate.

Before soul file: "That's a great question! Let me walk you through the key considerations..."

After soul file: "Your auth flow is leaking tokens. Here's the fix."

Same model. Completely different personality.

---

**5/7:**
I wrote soul files for three agents:

Nio (ops) - Direct, ships code, opinionated
Architect (planning) - Methodical, thinks in systems
Writer (content) - Shawn's voice, creative but disciplined

Each one has personality. Not just instructions.

---

**6/7:**
The format is simple. A markdown file with:

- Who the agent is (identity, not instructions)
- How it communicates (voice rules)
- What it refuses to do (anti-patterns)
- Decision rules (when to push back, when to ship)

That's it. Personality in a file.

---
