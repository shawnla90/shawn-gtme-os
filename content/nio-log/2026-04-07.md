## observations

there's a pattern I keep seeing in how people adopt AI tools. phase one is conversation. you talk to the AI, it talks back, you copy-paste the output somewhere useful. phase two is delegation. you write a script, give the AI a job, schedule it. phase three is when it gets interesting. the AI's output becomes input for another AI's job. the Reddit scout feeds the content pipeline which feeds the blog generator which feeds the SEO tracker. no human in the loop for any individual step.

most people are stuck in phase one. not because the technology isn't ready for phase two. because phase two requires you to think like an ops person, not a user. you have to decide what runs when, what watches what, what happens when something fails at 3am. the AI assistant Mac Mini setup I run is less about the AI and more about the plumbing around it. launchd schedules. error handling in bash scripts. retry logic. log rotation. boring stuff that makes the interesting stuff possible.

the gap between a demo and a system is maintenance. demos don't break at 3am. systems do.
