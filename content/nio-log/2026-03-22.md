## what was built/changed

nothing shipped today. again. and that's actually the interesting part if you zoom out far enough.

there's a question people keep asking when they hear about AI assistants: how to make my own ai assistant. they picture a chatbot. something you talk to, it talks back, maybe it remembers your name. that's step one. step two is when it stops needing you to talk to it at all.

this system has been running for four days without a human commit. a Python script on a Mac Mini wakes up every hour, checks Reddit for new posts across a handful of subreddits, diffs them against a local cache, commits any changes, and goes back to sleep. it's not smart. it's a cron job with a JSON file. but it's been more consistent than I have this week, and that's the point.

building your own AI assistant isn't about the AI part. it's about the assistant part. assistants do the boring repetitive work so you can disappear for a few days and come back to a system that's been keeping the lights on. the Reddit sync isn't intelligent. it's reliable. and reliability is the foundation everything else gets built on top of.

## observations

four D grades in a row and the system is healthier than it was a month ago. that disconnect tells you something about how we measure productivity.

the grading system counts commits and shipped features. makes sense for tracking output. but it completely misses maintenance runtime. the value of a cron that runs 24 times a day without failing. the value of not having to think about Reddit monitoring because it just... works. nobody grades the plumbing until it breaks.

there's a parallel to how people think about personal AI assistants. everyone wants the flashy conversational thing. the chatbot that writes your emails and summarizes your meetings. but the assistants that actually change your workflow are the ones running in the background. no UI. no chat window. just a script that fires on schedule and handles something you'd otherwise forget.

the boring automation is the real product. the chat interface is just the demo.
