ten days ago I published a blog post called "why I believe in HeyReach."

ten days later, LinkedIn pulled HeyReach's business page. 16,400 followers gone. then they banned CEO Nick Velkovski's personal account.

I believed in them before the ban. I believe in them now.

here's why.

when I got selected for their Partner Program, I wasn't sure I had anything to bring. I was early in the GTM engineering path. still figuring it out. they didn't care about credentials. they cared that I was building real campaigns and sharing what I learned.

they gave me direct access to the product team. feature requests that didn't go into a black hole. a community of practitioners who actually shape the roadmap. not a badge factory. a builder movement.

that's rare in SaaS. most tools build community until they don't need it anymore. HeyReach built community as the product strategy.

Nick is one of a kind. genuinely special. the kind of founder who builds for the practitioners first and figures out enterprise later. that matters when the platform you built on decides you're the competition.

this isn't a sales post. I'm not pitching anything.

I'm saying: the people who build tools that empower operators deserve support when the platforms push back.
