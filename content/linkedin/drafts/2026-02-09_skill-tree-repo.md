## Version 1: The Gamer Hook

this looks familiar.

Final Fantasy 10 skill tree. spent probably a hundred hours grinding through this thing when i was younger.

didn't know what i was doing. didn't know what building a skill tree looked like. but the gamification kept me hooked. the unlocks. the XP. the progression.

fast forward to last week.

i'm building a repo. didn't know what building a repo was when i started. didn't know what it looked like.

but here's what clicked: once you acquire these skills in the game, you own them. you can repeat them. you can use them in different combinations.

that's exactly what you're doing with your go-to-market system. your content system. even your ad system.

you're building a skill tree.

every MCP server you add? that's a new ability unlocked. every skill file you write? that's a node on your tree. every workflow you automate? that's a combo move.

it can be complicated. but hey, if you were a gamer, this is all for you.

this is your moment. this is your chance.

any GTM engineer or STR who fears these more advanced tools should know: either get in or get left behind.

at the same time, if you're somebody who's into RPGs or Dungeons & Dragons, this is your way in.

i'm sure i'm not the only one who sees that.

but here's the thing: if you're not a gamer, just realize that if you're out there grabbing MD skills from every lead magnet post you see, you're basically unlocking an insanely OP ability. like that Final Fantasy skill that lets you cast a massive fireball that hits three targets at once. or the clone ability that creates an army of copies that mirror your moveset.

you're collecting abilities. you're building your tree.

in a game, the tree is pre-built. in your repo, you're building it yourself.
