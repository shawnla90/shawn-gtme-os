not because i've been playing it recently. because i've been building one.

here's the pattern i didn't see until last week:

when you're grinding through a game, you don't know what the full tree looks like. you just unlock one node at a time. you experiment. you try combinations. you see what works.

that's exactly how i built my repo. one skill file at a time. one MCP server at a time. one workflow at a time.

didn't know what i was building. didn't know what it would look like when it was done.

but the gamification kept me going. the unlocks. the XP. the progression.

probably a hundred hours in the last seven days. cursor crazy.

and here's what clicked: once you acquire these skills in the game, you own them. you can repeat them. you can use them in different combinations.

that's exactly what you're doing with your go-to-market system. your content system. your ad system.

you're building a skill tree. and once you unlock those abilities, they're yours.

it can be complicated. but if you were a gamer, this is all for you. this is your moment.

you're collecting nodes for your tree. in a game, the tree is pre-built. in your repo, you're building it yourself.
