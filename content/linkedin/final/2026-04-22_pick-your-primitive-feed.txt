HubSpot's MCP went GA last week. Salesforce's is still beta. Salesforce just put MCP and CLI on equal footing at TDX with Headless 360.

Nobody's lining all three up in the same sentence.

I ran the numbers on my own stack before picking a side: 5.94 billion cache-read tokens in the last 65 days of Claude Code CLI. That ratio is only possible when you architect around one context, not chatty tool calls.

Which means the CLI-vs-MCP debate isn't really a debate. It's a role question.

MCP is a convenience layer for agents that can't code. It solves real problems for reps, enterprise admins, governance.

CLI is for agents that can. Builders who are the user, the admin, and the maintainer.

Salesforce just quietly named three first-class surfaces and stopped picking favorites. The primitive you pick decides what you can build.
