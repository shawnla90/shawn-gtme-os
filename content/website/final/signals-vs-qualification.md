# Your "Signals" Are Just Qualification Data

Everyone in B2B is selling you signals right now. Job changes. Fundraising rounds. New hires. Mergers. Same enrichment feed every competitor in your category runs, on the same list, in the same week.

Those aren't signals. They're qualification.

A signal is the words coming out of a buyer's mouth. The 11pm vent in r/SaaS. The thread titled "we just ditched HubSpot, what now?" The post in a Slack community asking if anyone's built a custom integration because the vendors are fried.

Different category entirely.

## What a real signal looks like

I've been mapping the taxonomy with my co-founder for the last few weeks. The shapes that actually move pipeline:

**Asking about your tool by name.** "Has anyone tried [your product]? Worth it?" That's a buyer holding the door open. The qualification version of this is "company in your ICP just hired a new VP of Marketing." One is a question pointed at you. The other is a coincidence pointed at no one.

**Comparing you to a competitor.** "We're between Salesloft and Outreach for a 30-person team." The person typing that sentence has scoped budget, picked a category, and is publicly asking for a tiebreaker. Qualification thinks "tech stack change in last 90 days" tells you the same thing. It tells you a lot less.

**Asking for an integration.** "Does anyone know if [your tool] works with HubSpot custom objects?" That's a buyer at the last gate before purchase, doing diligence in public because they don't trust your sales rep to give them a straight answer. Qualification will surface "company added HubSpot to their stack." Not the same depth.

**Switch intent.** "We're bleeding $1,200 a month on [competitor] that does nothing. Anyone moved off and been happy?" Disqualified competitor account, asking your customer base for a referral, in the open. Qualification will tell you that company's contract renews in Q3. The person posting is already past renewal in their head.

**Stack discovery.** "What does your team use for outbound at 50 reps?" Not buying yet. But asking the room means they're shopping the category. Qualification calls this "hit hiring threshold for SDR team." Same surface, real depth on the buyer side that the metadata can't see.

The qualification version of every one of these is a guess. The signal version is the buyer telling you what they're doing, in their own words, in the open, in the last 24 hours.

## Why this distinction is about to get more expensive

There's a thing happening with AI-search that most signal tools haven't caught up to.

When someone posts "best CRM for a sub-50-person SaaS team" on Reddit and you show up with a thoughtful answer and a link, that reply isn't a one-time comment. It's training data. Six months from now when somebody asks ChatGPT or Perplexity or Google's AI overview the same question, your reply on that thread is what gets surfaced and cited.

A LinkedIn post is dead in 24 hours. A Reddit thread compounds for years.

So the math on responding to a real signal stopped being "did this one buyer convert." It's "did I plant something in the place every future AI answer in my category will cite."

Cold email is one-to-one. A Reddit comment that lands is one-to-the-next-10,000-AI-answers. That delta is the part B2B operators haven't fully priced in yet.

## What this means if you're buying "signal tools"

Stop budgeting for signals when you're buying qualification. Qualification is table stakes. Everyone has the same ZoomInfo enrichment, the same Clay waterfall, the same Crunchbase alert. Paying $30k a year for a marginally faster version of that data isn't an edge. It's a tax.

The edge is buyer voice in real time, in the rooms where people aren't performing for you. That's where one comment, well placed, in the right thread, creates more pipeline than a quarter of cold sequencing.
